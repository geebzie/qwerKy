_DermatologyTerminology

_Hypoallergenic

There are no Federal standards or definitions that govern the use of the term "hypoallergenic."

_Non-comodogenic

Skincare product or cosmetic that is formulated so as not to cause blocked pores.

_Paraben-free

Parabens are esters (a compound formed from acid and alcohol) of p-hydroxybenzoic acid.

_Alcohol-free

In cosmetic and skincare labeling, the term "alcohol," used by itself, refers to ethyl alcohol. Cosmetic products, including those labeled "alcohol free," may contain other alcohols, such as cetyl, stearyl, cetearyl, or lanolin alcohol.

_Dermatologically tested

When a brand claims a product is 'dermatologically tested' it means they've tested it on human skin. However, it doesn't tell us what the tests undertaken were designed to show, or whether the product even passed them.
